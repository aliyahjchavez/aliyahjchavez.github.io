Recognition & Honors

The following highlights some of my media coverage,
my major accomplishments, and honors/awards I have received.

Graduate of Stanford University
Spring 2019

Graduated from Stanford University in California with a Master of Arts in Journalism, as well as a Bachelor of Arts in Communication and the Comparative Studies in Race and Ethnicity.

Profiled in Stanford's Daily Report before 2019 Commencement
Spring 2019

Chosen among thousands of students to be featured in this article, which highlights my achievements and struggles at Stanford, as well as future plans.

Featured in Indian Country Today promoting my hire
Spring 2019

An article highlighting my start at Indian Country Today post graduation as part of the inaugural Tribal Fellows Program.

Honored Reporter by Stanford Journalism
Spring 2019

Competing against 135 stories, my work at the West Valley View in Phoenix, AZ during the Summer of 2018 was highlighed at a journalism luncheon by one of the program's biggest donors.

Moderator with Urban Native Era
Fall 2018

Asked to moderate a conversation between Native artists at the Union Square Apple Store in San Francisco.

Selected reporter for first-ever Native Election night.
Fall 2018

Worked as part of a team that produced a 5-hour live television broadcast on election night to highlight the record number of Native Americans who ran for office.

Stanford Cheer Team
Spring 2017

Selected as a member of Stanford's varsity cheerleading squad.

Today Show Extra
Summer 2017

Selected by Today Show producers to be an extra on four segments aired nationwide.

Stanford Alternative Spring Break
Spring 2015

Traveled with a small group of students to the Rosebud Reservation in rural South Dakota to learn from teachers, doctors, tribal leaders and cultural practitioners about Indigenous Ways of Knowing by exploring the Lakota Narrative.

Gates Millenium Scholar
Spring 2014

Recipient of competitive scholarship program designed to promote academic excellence and to provide an opportunity for outstanding minority students to reach their highest potential through reducing financial barriers and developing future leaders.
