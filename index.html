About

I am a multimedia journalist with a dedication to telling important stories.

I am a proud member of Kewa Pueblo, one of the 21 Indigenous nations in New Mexico. Much of my inspiration stems from growing up in the Pueblo, a place where Native people are largely underrepresented in all forms of media.

Currently, I am a reporter and producer at Indian Country Today in Phoenix, Arizona. We are a digital news platform that covers news happening in Indigenous communities around the country. In the fall, we will be launching a television news show where I will continue my reporting. Previously, I have worked in national newsrooms including the Peninsula Press, the West Valley View, and NBCâ€™s Today show.

I graduated from Stanford University in 2019 with an M.A. in Journalism and a B.A. in Communication and the Comparative Studies in Race and Ethnicity. At Stanford, I was an active member of the Native American community, holding leadership positions with the powwow and Stanford American Indian Organization. I was also a member of the Stanford varsity cheerleading team where I cheered at various bowl games and NCAA postseason tournaments. I am a member of the Native American Journalists Association.

In my free time, I enjoy all things fitness and spending time with my family and loved ones.
