About

Aliyah Chavez is a multimedia journalist passionate about the
intersection of Indigenous communities and news.
She is a gifted storyteller and skilled in broadcast, digital media, and reporting.

Chavez is a member of the Kewa Pueblo and is passionate about her Native American heritage. Much of her inspiration stems from growing up on the Kewa Pueblo tribal reservation in New Mexico– a place where Native people are largely underrepresented in all forms of media.

Currently, Aliyah is currently using her passions for reporting the news and drawing attention to the issues of indigenous communities as a journalist for Indian Country Today in Phoenix, AZ. She works on a PBS program for Native American peoples created by Native American journalists. She has interned and worked freelance for Indian Country Today in the past, covering, for instance, an Indigenous Peoples Day event on Alcatraz Island and the mid-term congressional elections. Previously, she has completed internships with the U.S. Department of Interior, NBC’s TODAY show, and the West Valley View in Phoenix.

Chavez graduated from Stanford University in 2019 with an M.A. in Journalism. She also received a B.A. in Communication and the Comparative Studies in Race and Ethnicity from Stanford in 2018. At Stanford, she was involved in the Native American community, holding leadership positions with Powwow and the Stanford American Indian Organization and was on the varisity cheerleading team. She is also a member of the Native American Journalists Association. In her free time, she enjoys all things fitness, playing with her young siblings and cheering for the Stanford Cardinal.
